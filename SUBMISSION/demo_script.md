# Setu — Live Demo Script
*3 minutes. Then stop.*

---

## Structure at a Glance

```
0:00 – 0:35   The story        (no laptop, just you talking)
0:35 – 0:50   The problem      (one sentence, then silence)
0:50 – 2:30   Live demo        (working app, real audio)
2:30 – 3:00   The close        (one line, then stop)
```

**The goal isn't to explain Setu. It's to make at least one judge feel something.**

---

## Before You Walk Up

- App open at `http://localhost:8000` in Chrome, fullscreen (F11)
- Languages pre-set to **English ↔ Hindi** (or your chosen demo pair)
- Transcript cleared, session not yet started
- A second person ready — a friend, or ask a judge to volunteer
- Laptop plugged in, volume up, mic permission already granted
- MoodMap short film queued up if you plan to reference it

---

## 0:00 – 0:35 | The Story
*Don't open your laptop yet. Just talk. Look at them.*

> "Two years ago I built something called MoodMap on my personal laptop. I had no fancy
> resources — just a gaming machine, 5,000 audio samples from Kaggle, and one belief:
> that AI could hear people but it couldn't listen to them. I trained an emotion
> classifier, I wired it into an LLM, I even made a short film about it.
>
> And then I waited — because the idea was bigger than what I could build alone at the time.
>
> Today I stopped waiting."

*Now open the laptop.*

---

## 0:35 – 0:50 | The Problem
*One sentence. Then let it land. Don't rush to fill the silence.*

> "Every Indian family has a version of this: a grandmother who speaks Telugu, a
> granddaughter who speaks English, and a wall between them that nobody meant to build."

*Pause. Three full seconds. Let them picture their own family.*

---

## 0:50 – 2:30 | The Live Demo

### Setup line
*Show the screen — the dark UI, the Sanskrit header सेतु.*

> "This is Setu."

*Let them look at it for a moment.*

Click **Start Bridge**. Status dot goes green.

> "It's listening now. Not waiting for a button press — just listening, the way a real
> conversation works."

---

### Exchange 1 — Plain translation
*Speak simply, warmly, neutral tone:*

> "Did you eat? Come, sit with me."

Gemini translates into Hindi aloud. It appears in the transcript.

*Say nothing. Let it breathe for two seconds.*

---

### Exchange 2 — The moment that matters
*Now speak with real worry in your voice. A mother's voice. Urgent, a little strained:*

> "You look tired. You're not sleeping properly, are you? Tell me the truth."

When the golden **Setu notices** line appears on screen —

**Point at it. Stop talking.**

Wait three full seconds. Let them read it.

Then, quietly:

> "That's the thing no translator has ever done."

*This is your demo moment. Everything before this was setup.*

---

## 2:30 – 3:00 | The Close
*Short. Human. Don't summarize what they just saw.*

> "I spent two years believing AI could understand not just what people say, but what
> they mean. Setu is that belief, working.
>
> It's called Setu — a bridge. That's all it is."

**Stop. Don't say "thank you". Don't say "any questions?". Just stop.**

*The silence is part of it.*

---

## Q&A — Be Ready for These

**"How does the emotion detection actually work?"**
> "The Gemini Live API has a feature called Affective Dialog — when enabled, the model
> reads vocal tone, pace, and emotional register in real time, not just the words. I
> pair that with a system prompt that instructs Setu to notice when what someone says
> and how they say it don't match. The intelligence is in the model; the craft is in
> how I've directed it."

**"What makes this different from Google Translate?"**
> "Google Translate gives you the words. Setu gives you the person. Translation is the
> floor, not the ceiling."

**"Could this work as a real product?"**
> "I think about my own family when I think about this. The gap is real, it exists in
> millions of homes, and the only thing missing was a bridge that felt human enough
> that people would actually want to use it. That's what I tried to build today."

**"Why did you build this alone?"**
> "Because I've been thinking about this for two years. I didn't need more time to
> figure out what to build — I just needed the API to catch up."

---

## The One Thing to Remember in the Room

When the Setu notice appears on screen — that golden line surfacing the subtext —
**slow down, not up.**

Every instinct will be to explain it. Don't.

Point at it. Pause for three seconds. Then say the one line.

That silence is what makes the judges *feel* it instead of just understand it.

---

## Rehearsal Notes
- Run the full demo end-to-end at least 5 times before 3:15 PM
- Practice the worried voice — it has to sound real, not performed
- The pause after the Setu notice is the hardest part — it will feel too long. It isn't.
- If the app glitches during the live demo: don't apologize, don't panic. Say:
  *"Let me restart the bridge."* Click stop, click start, keep going. Judges have
  seen demos break — they watch how you handle it.
