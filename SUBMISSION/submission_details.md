## Full Project Description
*(For the submission form's longer description field — paste this as-is)*

India has 22 official languages. Most families have at least two of them at the dinner table. And what slowly pulls people apart isn't the lack of love — it's the wall between the words.

A grandmother worries in Telugu. Her granddaughter replies in English. The worry lands as nagging. The reply lands as coldness. Neither person meant what the other received.

Setu fixes that.

It uses the Gemini Live API to listen continuously to both people — not in turns, not with a button press, just the way a real conversation flows. It translates in real time. But more than that, it reads the tone underneath the words. When it hears worry masked as criticism, or affection expressed as scolding, it surfaces it gently on screen: *"She sounds worried, not angry."*

It's not a translator. It's an emotional interpreter.

Built on Gemini 2.5 Flash Live with Affective Dialog enabled — which means the model isn't just processing text, it's listening to how something is said: the pace, the strain, the weight in someone's voice. The bridge runs entirely through the Live API — no turn-taking, no typing, no latency that breaks the feeling of a real conversation.

This project came from a personal place. Two years ago I built something called MoodMap — a multimodal emotion recognition system I trained on a gaming laptop because I believed AI was missing something human. Setu is the idea MoodMap was always pointing toward, now finally buildable.

**Tech stack:** Gemini 2.5 Flash Live (Affective Dialog, Live Translate), FastAPI, Web Audio API
